About FREDDIE

FREDDIE is a portable, self-propelled water treatment system designed to eliminate mercury contamination in aquatic environments. It works by filtering and chemically neutralizing pollutants through a multi-layer structure, ensuring clean and safe water.

What Is It?

FREDDIE is an innovative solution designed to transform access to potable water in rural communities near artisanal mining zones. With its advanced technology and modular design, FREDDIE captures mercury from contaminated waters, creating a safer and healthier environment for people.

FREDDIE is an autonomous, portable, and self-propelled device that uses a multi-layer filtration system to chemically neutralize water contaminants, ensuring clean and safe water for consumption. Its modular and scalable design allows implementation in household or community tanks, adapting to local needs without requiring complex infrastructure.

We offer a sustainable, reliable, and easy-to-implement solution that protects not only communities but also the environment, contributing to the reduction of mercury pollution and promoting a healthier future for all.

Main Features of FREDDIE

FREDDIE transforms access to potable water, eliminating mercury contamination and protecting community health

Outer Layer

FREDDIE's outer layer is made of high-density polyethylene (HDPE) or medical-grade PVC, serving as the first barrier against large particles and sediments. With a PTFE (Teflon) coating, it prevents contaminant buildup and enables self-cleaning. Its uniform perforations (1-2 mm) and stainless steel (316L) mesh reinforcement ensure a steady water flow, impact resistance, and durable protection.

First Sub-Layer

In this initial purification stage, water passes through a combination of advanced materials. Sulfurized activated carbon adsorbs mercury and other chemical contaminants through specific reactions. Iron-modified zeolite exchanges ions and retains hazardous particles, while Amberlite resin efficiently captures heavy metals, especially mercury, even in low concentrations. Each material is optimized to work together, achieving highly efficient initial contaminant removal.

Second Sub-Layer

This sub-layer reinforces the purification process using zero-valent iron nanoparticles, which reduce ionic mercury to elemental mercury, chemically trapping it. Quartz sand filters suspended particles and distributes the water flow uniformly, and activated bentonite traps heavy metals within its layers while preventing the formation of preferential flow channels. Together, these technologies remove remaining contaminants before reaching the device’s core.

Chemical Core

The core is the heart of FREDDIE's chemical process. Here, water comes into contact with a specialized mixture of reactants: sodium thiosulfate, which forms stable complexes with mercury, removing it from the water; ferric chloride, which oxidizes elemental mercury and accelerates chemical reactions; and modified chitosan, which adsorbs heavy metals and stabilizes captured contaminants. These advanced reactions ensure that mercury is completely neutralized and retained.

Electrochemical System

FREDDIE incorporates an innovative electrochemical system consisting of a titanium anode with ruthenium oxide, which oxidizes elemental mercury to its ionic form, preparing it for removal; a carbon-activated cathode, which reduces unwanted ionic species and adsorbs contaminants; and an ion-selective membrane, which ensures controlled flow and separates chemical reactions, optimizing efficiency. This system not only enhances mercury removal but also stabilizes the treated water.

Propulsion and Self-Cleaning Layer

To maximize its reach, FREDDIE features small internal propellers that allow it to move autonomously in water bodies. This system ensures the device treats wider and harder-to-reach areas, maintaining a constant flow of contaminated water toward its filtering layers. Its portable design makes it an adaptable and efficient solution for various applications.
