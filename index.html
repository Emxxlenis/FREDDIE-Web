About FREDDIE

FREDDIE is a portable, self-propelled water treatment system designed to eliminate mercury contamination in aquatic environments. It works by filtering and chemically neutralizing pollutants through a multi-layer structure, ensuring clean and safe water.

What Is It?

FREDDIE is an innovative solution designed to transform access to potable water in rural communities near artisanal mining zones. With its advanced technology and modular design, FREDDIE captures mercury from contaminated waters, creating a safer and healthier environment for people.

FREDDIE is an autonomous, portable, and self-propelled device that uses a multi-layer filtration system to chemically neutralize water contaminants, ensuring clean and safe water for consumption. Its modular and scalable design allows implementation in household or community tanks, adapting to local needs without requiring complex infrastructure.

We offer a sustainable, reliable, and easy-to-implement solution that protects not only communities but also the environment, contributing to the reduction of mercury pollution and promoting a healthier future for all.

Main Features of FREDDIE

FREDDIE transforms access to potable water, eliminating mercury contamination and protecting community health

Outer Layer

FREDDIE's outer layer is made of high-density polyethylene (HDPE) or medical-grade PVC, serving as the first barrier against large particles and sediments. With a PTFE (Teflon) coating, it prevents contaminant buildup and enables self-cleaning. Its uniform perforations (1-2 mm) and stainless steel (316L) mesh reinforcement ensure a steady water flow, impact resistance, and durable protection.

First Filtration Sub-Layer

This sub-layer is designed to trap heavy metals and mercury. It combines sulfurized activated carbon, natural zeolite modified with iron, and Amberlite IRC748 ion-exchange resin. Together, these substances adsorb mercury and other contaminants through specific chemical reactions and ion-exchange processes.

Second Filtration Sub-Layer

This layer uses zero-valent iron nanoparticles (nZVI) to reduce ionic mercury to its elemental form, which is then trapped on the surface. It also includes treated quartz sand as a physical filtering medium and activated bentonite to trap heavy metals and prevent preferential water flow channels.

Chemical Core

The core consists of a polyethylene chamber containing a specialized mix of sodium thiosulfate, ferric chloride, and modified chitosan. These substances neutralize and stabilize mercury ions, forming non-toxic compounds safely trapped in the core.

Electrochemical System

This layer includes a titanium anode coated with ruthenium oxide, an activated carbon cathode, and an ion-selective membrane. The system oxidizes elemental mercury into its ionic form (Hg²⁺), facilitating its capture. The membrane ensures efficient separation of chemical reactions, optimizing purification.

Propulsion and Self-Cleaning Layer

FREDDIE features small propellers that allow autonomous movement in water, ensuring even coverage. Its spherical design and PTFE coating reduce residue buildup, minimizing maintenance and extending its lifespan.
